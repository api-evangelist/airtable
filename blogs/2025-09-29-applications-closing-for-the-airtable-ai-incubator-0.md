But there is a glaring gap between this effort and true impact; a recent study by MIT found that <a href="https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/?ref=blog.airtable.com"><u>95% of corporate generative AI pilots fail</u></a> to lead to measurable business outcomes.&#xa0;</p><p>Whether you call it a pilot, a proof of concept, or an initiative, most AI efforts don&#x2019;t make it over the finish line. The reasons for this failure vary&#x2014;lack of clarity, insufficient resources, or tools that don&#x2019;t live up to the hype. While the potential is real, too often teams struggle to translate ideas into solutions that work in practice.</p><h3 id="introducing-the-airtable-ai-incubator">Introducing, the Airtable AI incubator&#xa0;</h3><p>The Airtable AI Incubator is designed to give builders the space, guidance, and the right environment to turn bold ideas into working solutions.&#xa0;</p><p>&#x201c;To get into the program you have to have an idea,&#x201d; says Mikayla LaRosa, senior AI programs lead, who runs the incubator. &#x201c;Participants bring their biggest challenges and boldest visions, and the incubator provides the structure, mentorship, and platform to bring those ideas to life.&#x201d;</p><p><strong>Applications for the program close at 12pm ET, September 30. </strong><a href="https://academy.airtable.com/page/airtable-ai-incubator?ref=blog.airtable.com"><em><u>If you have an idea, apply today.</u></em></a></p><h3 id="the-four-questions-every-builder-must-answer">The four questions every builder must answer</h3><p>For teams eager to make AI work in the real world, the incubator offers a unique opportunity to experiment in a supportive, collaborative environment. Builders gain access to Airtable&#x2019;s platform and community, where they can test ideas, learn from peers, and get feedback from experts who understand both AI and business workflows.</p><p>To apply, builders must answer four questions designed to clarify vision and ensure readiness:</p><ul><li>If resources weren&#x2019;t a limitation, what&#x2019;s the one system or workflow you&#x2019;d design to completely solve your team&#x2019;s biggest challenge?</li><li>Which AI tools have you experimented with, and what&#x2019;s been your experience&#x2014;wins, frustrations, or surprises?</li><li>If you could deploy this app successfully, what would be the quantifiable impact to your team and/or company? How would it change the way your team works?</li><li>What&#x2019;s holding you back from building this today? (Is it time, skills, clarity&#x2014;or something else?)</li></ul><p>These questions aren&#x2019;t just hurdles&#x2014;they&#x2019;re thought starters. They help builders uncover what matters most and set the stage for meaningful transformation.</p><p>The result? Not just another AI project, but a working solution that drives measurable value.</p><h3 id="what-do-you-want-to-build">What do you want to build?&#xa0;</h3><p>If you&#x2019;ve been sitting on an idea&#x2014;or if your team has been experimenting with AI but hasn&#x2019;t yet made the leap&#x2014;this is your chance.
